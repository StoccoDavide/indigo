class ODEsolver : public handle¶

Class container for Runge-Kutta solvers of the system of Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) or index-0 Differential Algebraic Equations (DAEs). The user must simply define the Butcher tableau of the method, which defined as:

\[\begin{split} \begin{array}{c|c} \mathbf{c} & \mathbf{A} \\ \hline & \mathbf{b} & \hat{\mathbf{b}} \end{array} \end{split}\]

where \( \mathbf{A} \) is the Runge-Kutta matrix (lower triangular matrix):

\[\begin{split} \mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1s} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2s} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{s1} & a_{s2} & \dots & a_{ss} \end{bmatrix}, \end{split}\]

\( \mathbf{b} \) is the Runge-Kutta weights vector relative to a method of order \( p \) (row vector):

\[ \mathbf{b} = \left[ b_1, b_2, \dots, b_s \right], \]

\( \hat{\mathbf{b}} \) is the (optional) embedded Runge-Kutta weights vector relative to a method of order \( \hat{p} \) (usually \( \hat{p} = p−1 \) or \( \hat{p} = p+1 \)) (row vector):

\[ \hat{\mathbf{b}} = \left[ \hat{b}_1, \hat{b}_2, \dots, \hat{b}_s \right], \]

and \( \mathbf{c} \) is the Runge-Kutta nodes vector (column vector):

\[ \mathbf{c} = \left[ c_1, c_2, \dots, c_s \right]^T. \]

Subclassed by RKexplicit, RKimplicit

function project(x_tilde, t)¶

Project the ODEs system solution \( \mathbf{x} \) on the invariants/hidden constraints \( \mathbf{H} (\mathbf{x}, t) = \mathbf{0} \). The constrained minimization problem to be solved is:

\[ \textrm{minimize} \quad \dfrac{1}{2}\left(\mathbf{x} - \widetilde{\mathbf{x}}\right)^2 \quad \textrm{subject to} \quad \mathbf{H}(\mathbf{x}, t) = \mathbf{0}. \]

Solution Algorithm

Given the Lagrangian of the minimization problem of the form:

\[ \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{x}, \boldsymbol{\lambda}) = \frac{1}{2}\left(\mathbf{x} - \widetilde{\mathbf{x}}\right)^2 + \boldsymbol{\lambda} \cdot \mathbf{H}(\mathbf{x}, t). \]

The solution of the problem is obtained by solving the following:

\[\begin{split} \left\{\begin{array}{l} \mathbf{x} + \mathbf{JH}_\mathbf{x}^T \boldsymbol{\lambda} = \widetilde{\mathbf{x}} \\[0.5em] \mathbf{H}(\mathbf{x}, t) = \mathbf{0} \end{array}\right. \end{split}\]

Using the Taylor expansion of the Lagrangian:

\[ \mathbf{H}(\mathbf{x}, t) + \mathbf{JH}_\mathbf{x} \delta\mathbf{x} + \mathcal{O}\left(\left\| \delta\mathbf{x} \right\|^2\right) = \mathbf{0} \]

define the iterative method as:

\[ \mathbf{x} = \widetilde{\mathbf{x}} + \delta\mathbf{x}. \]

Notice that \( \delta\mathbf{x} \) is the solution of the linear system:

\[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{I} & \mathbf{JH}_\mathbf{x}^T \\[0.5em] \mathbf{JH}_\mathbf{x} & \mathbf{0} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \delta\mathbf{x} \\[0.5em] \boldsymbol{\lambda} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \widetilde{\mathbf{x}} - \mathbf{x} \\[0.5em] -\mathbf{H}(\mathbf{x}, t) \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

where \( \mathbf{JH}_\mathbf{x} \) is the Jacobian of the invariants/ hidden constraints with respect to the states \( \mathbf{x} \).

Parameters

  • x_tilde – The initial guess for the states \( \widetilde{\mathbf{x}} \).

  • t – The time \( t \) at which the states are evaluated.

Returns

The solution of the projection problem \( \mathbf{x} \).

function solve(t, x_0, varargin)¶

Solve the system of ODEs/DAEs and calculate the approximate solution.

Usage

Solve without the solution projection on invariants/hidden constraints and disabled verbose mode.

sol = obj.solve(t, x_0);

Solve with the solution projection on invariants/hidden constraints and disabled verbose mode.

sol = obj.solve(t, x_0, true);

Solve without the solution projection on invariants/hidden constraints and enabled verbose mode.

sol = obj.solve(t, x_0, false, true);

Plot the first component of the solution.

plot(t, sol(1,:));
Parameters

  • t – Time vector \( \mathbf{t} = \left[ t_0, t_1, \ldots, t_n \right]^T \).

  • x_0 – Initial states value \( \mathbf{x}(t_0) \).

  • project – [optional, default = false] Apply projection to invariants at each step.

  • verbose – [optional, default = \( \mathrm{false} \)] Activate vebose mode.

  • epsilon – [optional, default = \( 1.0\mathrm{e}3 \)] If \( || \mathbf{x} ||_{\infty} > \varepsilon \) the computation is interrupted.

Returns

A matrix \( \left[(\mathrm{size}(\mathbf{x}) \times \mathrm{size} (\mathbf{t})\right] \) containing the approximated solution \( \mathbf{x}(t) \) of the system of ODEs/DAEs.

function adapt_step(x_h, x_l, d_t)¶

Compute adaptive time step for the next advancing step according to the error control method. The error control method used is the local truncation error (LTE) method, which is based on the following formula:

\[ \left| \mathbf{x}_{k+1} - \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(h)} \right| \leq \dfrac{C \Delta t^{p+1}}{p+1} \left| \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(h)} - \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(l)} \right| \]

where \( \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(h)} \) is the approximation of the states at \( k+1 \)-th time step \( \mathbf{x_{k+1}}(t_{k}+\Delta t) \) with higher order method, \( \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(l)} \) is the approximation of the states at \( k+1 \)-th time step \( \mathbf{x_{k+1}}(t_{k}+\Delta t) \) with lower order method, \( C \) is a constant, and \( p \) is the order of the method.

To compute the suggested time step for the next advancing step \( \Delta t_{k+1} \), the following formula is used:

\[ \Delta t_{k+1} = \dfrac{C \Delta t^{p+1}}{p+1} \left| \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(h)} - \mathbf{x}_{k+1}^{(l)} \right|^{-\frac{1}{p+1}} \]
Parameters

  • x_h – Approximation of the states at \( k+1 \)-th time step \( \mathbf{x_{k+1}}(t_{k}+\Delta t) \) with higher order method.

  • x_l – Approximation of the states at \( k+1 \)-th time step \( \mathbf{x_{k+1}}(t_{k}+\Delta t) \) with lower order method.

  • d_t – Advancing time step \( \Delta t\).

Returns

The suggested time step for the next advancing step \( \Delta t_{k+1} \).

virtual function step(x_k, x_dot_k, t_k, d_t)¶

Compute a step using a generic integration method for a system of ODEs/DAEs of the form \( \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{x}', t) = \mathbf{0} \). The step is based on the following formula:

\[ \mathbf{x}_{k+1}(t_{k}+\Delta t) = \mathbf{x}_k(t_{k}) + \mathcal{S}(\mathbf{x}_k(t_k), \mathbf{x}'_k(t_k), t_k, \Delta t) \]

where \( \mathcal{S} \) is the generic advancing step of the solver.

Parameters

  • x_k – States value at \( k \)-th time step \( \mathbf{x}(t_k) \).

  • x_dot_k – States derivative at \( k \)-th time step \( \mathbf{x}' (t_k) \).

  • t_k – Time step \( t_k \).

  • d_t – Advancing time step \( \Delta t\).

Returns

The approximation of \( \mathbf{x_{k+1}}(t_{k}+\Delta t) \) and \( \mathbf{x}'_{k+1}(t_{k}+\Delta t) \).
